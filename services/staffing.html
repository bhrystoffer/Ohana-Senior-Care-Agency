Staff Types Available

Clinical and caregiving staff, coordinated through partner agencies.

All staff placed through Ohana's coordination are employed by or contracted with independent staffing partner agencies, each pre-screened and credentialed by the agency before any placement.

Registered Nurses (RN)

Supplemental RN coverage for acute and post-acute settings: skilled floor nursing, IV therapy, case management, wound care, and complex clinical support.

Licensed Vocational Nurses (LVN)

LVNs for medication administration, wound dressing, vital signs monitoring, and skilled care support under RN or physician supervision.

Certified Nursing Assistants (CNA)

Direct patient care, ADL support, repositioning, ambulation assistance, and restorative nursing activities in facility and residential settings.

Caregivers & Home Aides

Non-medical caregivers and Home Health Aides for home care agencies, assisted living, and residential care settings needing supplemental staffing.

Medical Assistants (MA)

Clinical office support, patient intake, vital signs, phlebotomy (where certified), and administrative patient care tasks for clinical settings.

Request Process

From request to placement.

Ohana handles coordination between your facility and our staffing partner agencies, keeping the process simple and the communication clear.

1
Facility Request

Submit your staffing need to Ohana, including shift type, staff classification, clinical requirements, required certifications, and timeline. Same-day and urgent requests are accepted.

2
Partner Agency Matching

Ohana identifies the appropriate staffing partner agency based on staff availability, geographic coverage, licensure requirements, and your facility's specific clinical needs.

3
Placement Coordination

The staffing agency manages the employment relationship, onboarding documentation, and shift scheduling. Ohana remains your coordination point of contact throughout the engagement.

What our staffing partners verify

  • California license — active and in good standing (BVNPT, BRN)
  • Criminal background check completed
  • Reference verification from prior clinical assignments
  • Skills assessment and competency documentation
  • CPR and BLS certification current
  • Liability and workers' compensation insurance maintained
All staff are employed by or contracted with independent staffing partner agencies, not Ohana. Employment, licensure compliance, and workers' compensation responsibility rests with the staffing agency. Ohana verifies partner agency credentialing standards before making referrals.
