Palliative vs. Hospice: Plain Language

Two distinct approaches to comfort-focused care, both coordinated through Ohana's licensed partner network.

Palliative Care

Palliative care is symptom-focused support that can begin at any stage of illness, alongside curative treatment. It addresses pain, fatigue, emotional distress, and quality of life concerns.

Hospice Care

Hospice care is end-of-life support for patients who have chosen comfort over curative treatment. Typically for patients with a prognosis of 6 months or less if the disease follows its natural course.

Levels of Care

Types of hospice care

Medicare-certified hospice includes four levels of care. The appropriate level is determined by the patient's clinical needs and managed by the partner hospice agency, not by Ohana.

Routine Home Care

Level 1

Routine Home Care

Routine home care is the most common hospice level and may support comfort-focused care in a home, assisted living, board and care, or skilled nursing setting when appropriate.

Continuous Home Care

Level 2

Continuous Home Care

Continuous home care may be arranged during short periods of crisis when a patient needs more intensive support to manage symptoms in the current care setting.

General Inpatient Care

Level 3

General Inpatient Care

General inpatient care may be appropriate when symptoms cannot be managed in the current setting and short-term care in an inpatient environment is needed.

Respite Care

Level 4

Respite Care

Respite care may provide short-term relief for family caregivers while the patient receives temporary hospice-supported care in an approved setting.

Ohana Senior Care Agency helps coordinate hospice and palliative care access through independent licensed provider partners. Eligibility, level of care, coverage, authorization, and availability vary by provider, plan, location, and clinical assessment. Ohana does not provide hospice or palliative clinical services directly.
