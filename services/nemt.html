Our Fleet

Vehicle fleet

We operate and maintain our own vehicles, which means consistent quality, real accountability, and no dispatch middleman between you and a reliable ride.

Ambulatory Transport

Door-to-door transport for patients who are mobile but medically fragile, at fall risk, or cognitively impaired. Driver escort service is included. Patients are assisted from door to vehicle and from vehicle to their destination. No rideshare, no unassisted drop-off.

Wheelchair-Accessible Vehicles

ADA-compliant vehicles equipped with ramp or lift access for wheelchair-dependent patients. Tie-downs and securement systems meet federal and California safety standards. Attendant assistance provided during boarding and disembarking.

Gurney / Stretcher Transport

For non-ambulatory patients who must remain recumbent during transport, including post-surgical patients, those with severe mobility impairment, and patients transferring between facilities while receiving continuous care. Fully equipped for safe horizontal transport.

FAQ

Common questions about NEMT

Answers to the questions hospitals, dialysis centers, SNFs, and families ask most often about Ohana's medical transportation service.

Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT) is ground transport for patients who need a ride to or from a medical appointment but do not require emergency medical intervention during the trip. Ambulances are dispatched for life-threatening emergencies and are staffed with paramedics or EMTs providing active medical care in transit. Ohana NEMT provides safe, supervised transport for medically fragile or mobility-impaired patients, not for emergency response. For any life-threatening emergency, always call 911.

Ohana operates three vehicle types to meet different patient needs: ambulatory transport vehicles for patients who are mobile but medically fragile or cognitively impaired, wheelchair-accessible vehicles (WAV) with ADA-compliant ramp or lift systems for wheelchair-dependent patients, and gurney/stretcher transport vehicles for non-ambulatory patients who must remain recumbent during the trip. All vehicles are regularly inspected, fully insured, and California NEMT certified.

We prefer at least 24 hours' notice for standard bookings, which allows us to confirm vehicle type, driver, and routing. Confirmation is provided within 2 hours of the request during business hours. Same-day transport is accommodated based on fleet availability. Call our dispatch line directly for urgent or same-day needs and we will communicate availability honestly and quickly. Recurring standing transport for dialysis or regular appointments can also be set up on a scheduled basis.
