Ohana-Direct NEMT

Clean. Accessible. On time.

Ohana operates and maintains its own fleet, inspected and insured to California's NEMT standards. Every vehicle is outfitted to transport ambulatory, wheelchair-dependent, and non-ambulatory patients safely and comfortably.

  • Ambulatory, wheelchair, and gurney-equipped vehicles
  • ADA-compliant ramp and lift access
  • Fully licensed and insured — California NEMT certified
  • Regularly inspected and maintained
Our Fleet

Vehicle fleet

We operate and maintain our own vehicles, which means consistent quality, real accountability, and no dispatch middleman between you and a reliable ride.

Ambulatory Transport

Door-to-door transport for patients who are mobile but medically fragile, at fall risk, or cognitively impaired. Driver escort service is included. Patients are assisted from door to vehicle and from vehicle to their destination. No rideshare, no unassisted drop-off.

Wheelchair-Accessible Vehicles

ADA-compliant vehicles equipped with ramp or lift access for wheelchair-dependent patients. Tie-downs and securement systems meet federal and California safety standards. Attendant assistance provided during boarding and disembarking.

Gurney / Stretcher Transport

For non-ambulatory patients who must remain recumbent during transport, including post-surgical patients, those with severe mobility impairment, and patients transferring between facilities while receiving continuous care. Fully equipped for safe horizontal transport.

Who We Serve

Built for healthcare settings

Ohana NEMT is designed around the operational reality of medical facilities, not the convenience of a consumer app. Reliability, documentation, and clinical context are built into how we work.

Dialysis Centers

Reliable recurring transport for dialysis patients: scheduled routes, consistent driver assignment where possible, and a low no-show rate that dialysis coordinators can actually count on. We understand the stakes of missed dialysis appointments and treat on-time performance accordingly.

Hospitals & Discharge Coordinators

Safe, compliant discharge transport with direct coordination with your team on timing, patient condition, and documentation requirements. We work within your discharge workflow, not against it. Confirmation provided, driver details shared in advance, and changes accommodated with a single call.

Skilled Nursing Facilities

Inter-facility transfers, specialist appointments, outpatient therapy, and follow-up visits, coordinated to your scheduling requirements. We communicate directly with your care coordinators and can accommodate routine standing orders for patients with recurring transport needs.

Home-to-Appointment

For patients and families who need consistent, reliable medical transport from home to physician appointments, outpatient procedures, imaging, or therapy. Caregiver or family member may accompany the patient at no additional charge when space permits.

Coverage

Where we operate

Ohana NEMT currently serves Los Angeles County, Orange County, Riverside County, and San Bernardino County. Coverage extends to major medical centers, outpatient clinics, dialysis facilities, and residential care settings throughout the region.

Our dispatch team manages routing across all four counties, with the ability to handle cross-county transport for inter-facility transfers and specialist appointments at regional medical centers.

Service availability varies by location and scheduling. Contact our dispatch team to confirm coverage for your specific origin and destination.

Counties served

  • Los Angeles County — including LA Metro, San Gabriel Valley, South Bay, and Long Beach areas
  • Orange County — including Anaheim, Santa Ana, Irvine, and coastal communities
  • Riverside County — including Riverside, Moreno Valley, Temecula, and Palm Springs corridor
  • San Bernardino County — including San Bernardino, Ontario, Fontana, and High Desert communities
Booking

Scheduling transport

We keep the booking process straightforward. Standard requests go through our intake line or online form. Urgent needs get a direct line to dispatch.

Standard Scheduling

Request transport at least 24 hours in advance through our intake line or online form. Confirmation provided within 2 hours of request during business hours. All confirmations include driver details and estimated arrival window.

  • 24-hour advance notice preferred
  • Confirmation within 2 hours
  • Recurring schedules available for standing transport needs

Same-Day & Urgent

Same-day transport requests are accommodated based on fleet availability. For urgent medical transport needs, call us directly; we prioritize clinical urgency and will communicate availability honestly and quickly.

  • Same-day requests accepted subject to availability
  • Clinical urgency prioritized in dispatch queue
  • Direct dispatch line for facility partners

Ohana NEMT is non-emergency medical transportation only. For life-threatening emergencies, call 911.

Facility Partners

Transport coordination for facilities

Discharge planners and facility administrators can set up a facility account for streamlined NEMT coordination. Reduce administrative friction, eliminate the chase for transport confirmation, and give your patients a more reliable discharge experience.

Facility accounts include a dedicated point of contact, documentation support for compliance purposes, and consistent driver assignment where scheduling allows. No per-request phone tag. Submit through a single channel and we handle coordination.

Set Up Facility Account

What facility accounts include

  • Dedicated Ohana point of contact for all transport coordination
  • Streamlined intake: single channel for all transport requests
  • Consistent driver assignment for recurring patients where possible
  • Transport documentation support for discharge records
  • Proactive communication on driver ETA and any delays
  • Monthly transport summaries available on request

Ohana NEMT operates in compliance with California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) transportation requirements. Service is non-emergency medical transportation only, not ambulance service. For medical emergencies, call 911.
